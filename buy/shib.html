What is Shiba Inu?

Shiba Inu is touted as one of several digital assets that belong to the “meme coins” group, as their creation is inspired by popular Internet jokes. Despite a frivolous association with an embarrassed Shiba Inu dog named Kabosu, today, Shib ranks among the top 20 cryptos by market capitalization. A joke went too far.

Shiba Inu (Shib) crypto, often dubbed as the "Dogecoin killer," burst into the cryptocurrency scene with much excitement and cuteness. Launched in August 2020, Shiba Inu is an ERC-20 token that operates on the Ethereum blockchain. It started with a total supply of one quadrillion Shiba coins—making it far more prevalent than other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin.

The Shiba Inu, a cute Japanese dog breed, serves as the playful mascot of this crypto. What makes Shiba Inu unique is that it has its own decentralized exchange called ShibaSwap, where holders can trade Shib and even stake Shiba tokens to earn rewards.

From its humble beginnings, the Shib price has experienced both highs and lows, with some wild fluctuations along the way. It's always a thrilling ride for crypto investors, as they watch Shib coins go to the moon and then sometimes face the reality of dips. Despite these ups and downs, Shiba Inu has managed to get a firm sitting among the world’s largest cryptocurrencies by market cap.

Doge vs. Shib

Dogecoin and Shiba Inu have some distinctive differences. Shiba Inu, unlike Dogecoin, is built on the Ethereum blockchain, giving it the capability to handle more complex financial transactions.

Additionally, Shiba Inu operates on a proof-of-stake blockchain, which means it doesn't involve crypto mining like Dogecoin does using a proof-of-work blockchain, similar to Bitcoin. While these technicalities can be quite intricate, the important point for most users is that with Shiba Inu, you can stake your tokens and earn additional rewards.

Shiba Inu doesn't shy away from embracing its meme origins, while striving to establish a balance between utility and irreverence. For instance, instead of a traditional "whitepaper," Shiba Inu refers to its mechanics and economics document as a "woofpaper." When you stake Shiba Inu, it's referred to as "burying" your tokens. Furthermore, when you lend out your tokens to power the decentralized exchange ShibaSwap, it's called "digging."

In addition to Shib, the project’s native token, there are two companion tokens, "Leash" and "Bone," which are relatively more scarce and are intended to incentivize individuals to support and maintain the Shiba Inu network.

Who Created Shiba Inu Coin?

Ryoshi is the anonymous creator of Shiba Inu (Shib), a digital meme coin that launched in 2020 and stirred a lot of buzz in the crypto landscape. This secret personality is known to have sent nearly a half of Shib supply to Vitalik Buterin who, in return, burnt these coins to prevent inflation.

There is not much information about the father of Shiba Inu Coin. In one of his tweets, he describes himself as:

“I am a nobody, I am not important. The efforts to unmask my “identity” even if successful would be underwhelming. I am just some guy of no consequence tapping at a keyboard and I am replaceable. I am Ryoshi.”

Notably, in May 2022, Ryoshi suddenly vanished from social media and deleted all his posts on Twitter. The project’s community, known as the Shib Army, suggests that the reason for this disappearance was tensions between Ryoshi and Shiba Inu’s other team.

Although there was a short-term panic among Shiba investors and even acquisitions of Shiba Inu Token to be a mere rugpull, the Shib Army rallied around the remaining lead developer, Shytoshi Kusama, and refused to abandon the project.

Despite everything, the altcoin has managed to grow into one of the leading cryptocurrencies in the blockchain industry, boasting a significant market capitalization. The Shiba Inu ecosystem is flourishing today. It encompasses various essential blockchain tools, such as ShibaSwap and Shibarium, along with its own metaverse and NFT collection, known as the Shiboshis.

How Is Shib's Market Price Determined?

Shiba Inu's market price is primarily determined by the enthusiasm and sentiment of other investors in the crypto market. As a meme coin, Shiba Inu derives its value from the hype and excitement surrounding the coin rather than fundamental factors or utility.

Another factor that can impact the price of Shiba Inu is the anonymity and disappearance of its creators. The mysterious nature of the coin's creators can create intrigue and speculation among investors, potentially affecting its value in either negative or positive direction.

The total supply of one quadrillion tokens also plays a role in determining the price. The large supply of Shib tokens can create a perception of abundance, which may badly affect the perceived value of each individual token.

The performance of other major cryptocurrencies can influence the price of Shiba Inu. If popular coins like Bitcoin or Ethereum experience significant price movements, it can create a ripple effect in the broader cryptocurrency market, including Shib.

However, advancements in Shiba's protocol and ecosystem can cause a positive effect on its price. If the project releases innovative features, partnerships, or developments that attract attention and bring utility to the coin, it can lead to increased demand and potentially push up the price.

Where to Store your Shiba Inu Coins?

A cryptocurrency wallet serves as a container for your private keys – secret codes that grant you access to the Shiba Inu blockchain and allow you to transfer your funds. Cryptocurrency wallets vary depending on their security capabilities, multi-coin support, and embedded features.

  • Mobile Wallets: smartphone applications that provide quick and easy access to Shiba Inu tokens on the go. Such digital wallets offer user-friendly interfaces and support features like QR code scanning for seamless transactions.

  • Online Wallets: web-based platforms allowing storage and access to Shiba Inu through a browser. Such a wallet offers convenience and accessibility but is more susceptible to security risks.
